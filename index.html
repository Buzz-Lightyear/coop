The CO-OP Experience

We know how to run a great co-op program. In fact, most of our full time employees actually had co-ops with us. We truly want to help develop the skills that make you hire-able, preferably by us!

A co-op experience with RIDGID Software Solutions will give you experience with tons of great technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, responsive web design, Object Oriented Programming and high level languages (e.g. C#, Java, Objective C, etc.), data persistence (e.g. Relational and Document Store/NoSQL Databases), web services, unit testing, Test Driven Development (TDD), Acceptance Test Driven Development (ATDD), and the list goes on and on!

In order to maximize the co-op experience, we strongly prefer that you have more than one co-op experience with us. This enables you to get an optimal experience with different projects, technologies, and team members so you can explore the many facets of software development and discover what you really love to do.

What we're looking for


RIDGID offers awesome co-op opportunities for Software Engineering, Quality Assurance (QA), User Experience (UX), and other blended roles. We are looking for creative individuals who enjoy collaboration, are excited about software development, who love to learn and share, and who want their work to make an impact. If you are proud to be a geek and have an interest in any of these disciplines then you’ll be a great fit!

How to Apply?

If you're interested in applying for a Co-Op experience at RIDGID, please follow the steps below and we'll follow up with you.

Pick one of these:

  • Send us a video stating why you think you’d be a good candidate for a co-op experience with us.
  • Tell us more about yourself and why you’d be a good candidate in writing (typing is fine).

Move yourself to the top of the list with one of these:

  • Put some of your code out on GitHub or via a Gist and share it with us.
  • Show us you’ve got some nerd cred – walk us through your Stack Exchange (or GitHub or something similar) profile and tell us why you are proud of it.
  • Solve any Project Euler problem and send us your solution.

Email your submission

Just email your submission to jacob.gordon@emerson.com and we'll get back to you!
